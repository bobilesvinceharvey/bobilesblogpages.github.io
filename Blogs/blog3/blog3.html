Where I currently am

My current skills are laughable, and honestly pathetic. I can code, I know the syntax, but compared to the people who are on the same level, I really do need to work harder, practice more, ask more, study more, and most importantly, code more.

Currently, I know how to code in Python, Java, SQL, and C++. But I am currently more familiar with C++ since it is the latest language we needed to study. I also know how to write HTML albeit having a long way to go.

I know within myself that I may know the basics of these languages, and maybe some complex parts of them, but I still have a lot to learn and improve on if I really want to become a software engineer. I need a whole lot of practice before I graduate and I worry that I am losing time.

My main goal is to become a software engineer. I plan to improve my coding skills by practicing consistently, taking on more complex projects, and re-learning my previous programming languages. I also want to gain experience in software development through more personal projects. I am determined and have to be even more determined to keep learning and growing in this field. Plans:

  • Think of problems I currently have and make projects out of them to show interviewers that I can fix problems and do actual work.
  • Network with professionals or even fellow students in the industry to learn from their experiences and gain insights into the field.
  • Fix my sleeping schedule.

Goals:

  • Become proficient in at least two programming languages.
    • Re-learn Python or Java.
    • Improve my skills in C++.
  • Build a portfolio of projects that showcase my skills and experience of at least 5 personal projects.

  • Train how I speak to make sure I get to relay what I really want to say and avoid misunderstandings especially in work interviews.
